What this lab is about

DNA must fit inside the nucleus, so cells package it in several levels of folding. This packaging changes how accessible genes are, which affects gene expression, DNA repair, and chromosome behavior during cell division.

A karyotype is a visual arrangement of chromosomes by size, shape, and banding pattern. It is used to identify chromosome number changes, sex chromosome patterns, and major structural abnormalities such as deletions, duplications, translocations, and extra chromosomes.

Cancer, chromosome errors, and packaging

Cancer cells often show chromosomal instability. This can include aneuploidy, translocations, deletions, duplications, and abnormal chromosome structure.

Aneuploidy means an abnormal chromosome number. It can arise when chromosomes do not separate correctly during meiosis or mitosis. That mis-separation is called nondisjunction.

Chromatin packaging also matters in cancer because genes controlling cell division can become misregulated if chromatin is altered or if DNA repair fails.

Important connection: DNA damage is easier to repair when DNA is more open and accessible. Extremely condensed DNA is harder for repair proteins to access.
