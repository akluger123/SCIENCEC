Step 1: UV Radiation Creates a Thymine Dimer

UV-B radiation from the sun penetrates the skin and strikes DNA, causing adjacent thymine bases to fuse into an abnormal structure called a thymine dimer — distorting the double helix.

📸 Simulation Diagram — UV-B Striking DNA

☀️ What is UV-B?

UV-B (280–315 nm wavelength) is a form of ultraviolet radiation emitted by the sun. It penetrates the outer skin layers and is energetic enough to be absorbed directly by DNA bases. UV-A also reaches Earth but UV-B is the primary cause of direct DNA damage.

🔗 The Thymine Dimer

When two adjacent thymine (T) bases on the same DNA strand absorb UV-B energy, they form an abnormal covalent bond between them — fusing into a single unit called a cyclobutane pyrimidine dimer (CPD). This is sometimes called a "snowman" structure.

🌀 Helix Distortion

The fused dimer bulges outward, bending and distorting the double helix. This physical kink disrupts the normal base-pairing geometry, making it impossible for enzymes to read the DNA sequence correctly at that location.

Step 2: The Cell Cannot Read Its Own DNA

🔵 RNA Polymerase Function

RNA Polymerase reads the DNA template strand in the 3'→5' direction and builds a complementary mRNA strand in the 5'→3' direction. Each codon (3-base unit) in mRNA codes for a specific amino acid in the resulting protein.

🚧 Why It Stalls

The thymine dimer creates a physical bulge and distortion in the helix. RNA Polymerase cannot "read" the fused bases because they no longer have the correct hydrogen-bonding geometry to serve as a template. The enzyme physically cannot advance past the lesion.

🎯 Cancer Connection

If the damaged gene is a tumor suppressor gene (like p53 or BRCA1) or a proto-oncogene, the resulting faulty protein can cause cells to divide uncontrollably — the definition of cancer at the cellular level.

Step 3: Nucleotide Excision Repair (NER)

The cell's most important UV-damage repair system. Four precise phases: Detect → Excise → Rebuild → Seal. A healthy cell can repair thousands of dimers per day.
